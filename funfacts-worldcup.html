World Cup Fun Facts

The Smallest World Cup Winner

Uruguay, the host nation of the first World Cup in 1930, had a population of just 1.7 million people at the time - making them the smallest country to ever win the tournament.

The Longest Goal in World Cup History

In the 1994 World Cup, Brazilian goalkeeper Rene Higuita scored a stunning goal from his own half of the field against Scotland, setting the record for the longest goal scored in a World Cup match.

The Youngest World Cup Player

In the 1950 World Cup, Norman Rimmington of England became the youngest player to ever appear in a World Cup match at the age of just 17 years and 93 days.

The Longest World Cup Match

The 2006 World Cup quarter-final between Germany and Argentina went to a penalty shootout, which lasted for 18 kicks - the longest penalty shootout in World Cup history.

The Most Successful World Cup Team

Brazil has won the FIFA World Cup a record 5 times, in 1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, and 2002, cementing their status as the most successful national team in the tournament's history.

The Highest-Scoring World Cup Match

The 1954 World Cup quarter-final between Austria and Switzerland ended with a score of 7-5, making it the highest-scoring match in World Cup history.
